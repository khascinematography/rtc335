This paper aims to provide a simple yet accurate set of key points

EXPOSURE VARIABLES

• the length (time) of exposure (shutter)
• the lens opening (aperture)
• the speed of the film (ISO)

indirect variable: frame rate (fps)
independent variable: scene luminance. It is common practice to measure illuminance (lux, fc), even though sensors respond to luminance.

LIGHT AND EXPOSURE STOPS

Doubling or halving the light increases or decreases the exposure by a stop:

  • The change from ISO 400 to 800 is one stop, from 800 to 1600 another, and so on.
  • The change from 1/50 to 1/100 is one stop, from 1/100 to 1/200 another, and so on.
  • The change from 45° to 90° is one stop, from 90° to 180° another, and so on.
  • Shutter speed and ISO scales typically use one-third stop increments.

Following are the practical rounded one-third stop increments:

ISO: 100, 125, 160, 200, 250, 320, 400, 500, 640, 800, 1000...
Exposure Time: 1/50, 1/60, 1/80, 1/100, 1/125, 1/160, 1/200, 1/250, 1/320...

F-NUMBER SEQUENCE

Unlike shutter speed or ISO, which change in linear steps, f-numbers follow a logarithmic sequence: each value is √2 times the previous, because doubling the light requires doubling the aperture area, which is proportional to the square of its diameter.

f/1.0 = √(2)0, f/1.4 = √(2)1, f/2.0 = √(2)2, f/2.8 = √(2)3...

Following are the practical rounded one stop increment f-numbers:

f/1.0, f/1.4, f/2.0, f/2.8, f/4.0, f/5.6, f/8, f/11, f/16, f/22

SHUTTER SPEED - SHUTTER ANGLE

• In cinematography, shutter angle is used instead of shutter speed.
• Most consumer-level hybrid cameras use shutter speed.
• A half shutter angle (180°) opening provides natural motion blur.

Shutter Speed - Shutter Angle Conversion

Shutter Angle to Shutter Speed
frame rate x (360 / shutter angle)
e.g. (25 x (360 / 180)) = 50 (1/50)

Shutter Speed to Shutter Angle
frame rate x shutter interval x 360
e.g. (25 x (1/50) x 360) or (25 x (360/50)) = 180°

FLICKER-FREE SHOOTING

• For flicker-free shooting, it's important to consider the power frequency of the country.
• The two main power frequencies are 50Hz and 60Hz.

Flicker-Free Shutter Angle Calculation

frame rate x (360 / power frequency)
e.g. (25 x (360 / 50)) = 172.8°

LIGHT METERING

• There are two types of metering: spot and incident.
• Spot metering measures light reflected from the subject (reflectance).
• Incident metering measures light falling onto the subject (ambient illumination).
• Most digital cameras have built-in spot meters.
• Spot meters assume a subject reflectance of 18%.
• Average caucasian skin reflects about 36% (1 EV > 18%).
• White paper peaks around 90% (≈2.3 EV > 18%).
• Spot measurement for reflectance values other than 18% should be compensated.
• Simplest way to set ‘correct exposure’ is by using an 18% gray card.

Example Compensation

When measuring a 90% reflectance target, you must compensate the exposure by ≈2.3 stops; otherwise, scene white will be rendered as 18% gray.
